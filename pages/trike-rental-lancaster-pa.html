No Motorcycle License Needed in Pennsylvania

This surprises most people. In Pennsylvania, the Polaris Slingshot is legally classified as an autocycle — not a motorcycle. You do not need a motorcycle endorsement or license. Your standard PA driver's license (or any valid out-of-state license) is all that's required.

The Can-Am Spyder F3 is also classified as an autocycle in Pennsylvania for the same reason. So no matter which trike you pick, there's no special paperwork, no endorsement, and no barrier to booking. If you can drive a car, you can rent a trike from CJ's.

Best Trike Routes in Lancaster, PA

Lancaster County has some of the most scenic back roads in the entire mid-Atlantic — ideal for a trike. Here's where our renters go:

  • Amish Country — Route 340 — The most popular trike route from Lancaster. Bird-in-Hand, Intercourse, Strasburg, and Ronks. Rolling farmland, horse-drawn buggies, no stoplights. 40–60 miles of pure scenery.
  • Susquehanna River Road (PA-441) — Head north along the river from Columbia to Marietta and Elizabethtown. One of the best river roads in Pennsylvania — cliffs, trees, and almost no traffic.
  • Covered Bridge Scenic Byway — Lancaster County has 28 covered wooden bridges. This loop takes you through the best ones via Strasburg, Pequea, and Martic townships. 50–70 miles, excellent scenery.
  • Gettysburg Battlefield — 45 minutes west of Lancaster on US-30. Ride the 24-mile battlefield auto tour through the historic grounds. A once-in-a-lifetime trike experience.
  • Holtwood Dam Loop — Southeast Lancaster County, along the Susquehanna at Holtwood. Dramatic cliffs, a historic covered bridge, and a dam overlook. One of the most underrated routes in the county.
