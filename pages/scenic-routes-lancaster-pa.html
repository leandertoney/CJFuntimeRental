Lancaster, PA · Ride Guide

The Best Scenic Rides
from Lancaster, PA

Lancaster puts you at the center of some of the most varied riding roads in the Mid-Atlantic. River roads, farmland loops, covered bridges, and hill climbs — all within 30 miles of the city. Here are 6 routes worth doing on a Polaris Slingshot or Can-Am Spyder.

01
River Loop · Half Day

Lancaster to Holtwood & Susquehanna Loop

~55
Miles
2–3 hrs
Total Time

This is the locals' favorite. You drop south out of Lancaster toward the Susquehanna River and follow it through some of the most dramatic gorge scenery in the county. The road narrows, the tree canopy closes in, and by the time you reach Holtwood Dam you've left the city behind completely.

The Holtwood section runs along the river on River Road — a twisting, tree-lined stretch that hugs the bank of the Susquehanna. There are overlooks, a nature preserve, and almost no traffic mid-week. Coming back north through Safe Harbor and Columbia closes the loop on a high note: the Columbia-Wrightsville bridge over the river is one of the most iconic crossings in Central PA.

This ride is best done in the late morning to catch the light on the river. Pack a stop at the Holtwood overlook — it's one of the best views in Lancaster County.

Route Lancaster → PA-324 South → River Road → Holtwood → Safe Harbor → PA-441 North → Columbia → PA-462 East → Lancaster
Distance ~55 miles round trip
Drive Time 2–3 hours with stops
Best For River scenery, gorge roads, wildlife, half-day ride

Highlights

  • Holtwood Dam overlook — Susquehanna gorge views
  • River Road — narrow, winding, tree-lined
  • Safe Harbor marina and boat launch
  • Columbia-Wrightsville bridge crossing
02
Amish Country · Half Day

Amish Country Back Roads — Route 340 & Columbia River Road

~50
Miles
2 hrs
Total Time

Route 340 — "Old Philadelphia Pike" — is the spine of Lancaster County's Amish Country. It runs east-west through the heart of the county connecting Bird-in-Hand, Intercourse, and Smoketown. Horse-drawn buggies share the road on weekends, so this one is more about the scenery and pace than the speed.

The best version of this loop adds the Columbia River Road portion on the way back: head west toward Columbia on PA-462, then drop down to the river for a stretch before completing the loop. You get farmland going out, river coming back — two completely different landscapes in under 50 miles.

Stop at one of the farm stands along Route 340 for fresh produce and baked goods. The Intercourse area has multiple stands and a market — good midpoint break before you continue west.

Route Lancaster → PA-340 East → Bird-in-Hand → Intercourse → PA-772 West → Leola → PA-23 West → Columbia → River Road South → PA-462 East → Lancaster
Distance ~50 miles
Drive Time 2 hours with stops
Best For Amish Country scenery, farm stands, first-time riders

Highlights

  • Route 340 Amish farmland — buggies, barns, and open fields
  • Bird-in-Hand Farmers Market (Tues, Fri, Sat)
  • Intercourse roadside stands and baked goods
  • Columbia-Wrightsville bridge river crossing
03
Creek Ride · Half Day

Conestoga Run

~40
Miles
1.5–2 hrs
Total Time

The Conestoga Run is a local gem — a ride that follows the Conestoga River south and west from Lancaster through some of the county's most wooded and rural terrain. It's a route that locals do on motorcycles and rarely gets the attention it deserves from visiting riders.

The road hugs the creek for much of the run, alternating between tight tree-lined stretches and open river flats. The southern section toward Martic Forge and Pequea is particularly good — forested, quiet, and completely off the tourist circuit. This is the anti-Route 340: fewer buggies, fewer cars, and more road to yourself.

This one pairs well with the Holtwood loop — start with the Conestoga Run heading south, then swing east along the river to Holtwood for a longer half-day combination ride.

Route Lancaster → PA-324 South → Conestoga Road → Martic Forge → Pequea → Conestoga → PA-324 North → Lancaster
Distance ~40 miles
Drive Time 1.5–2 hours
Best For Wooded back roads, off the tourist path, creek views

Highlights

  • Conestoga River road sections — tree canopy, quiet roads
  • Martic Forge historic area
  • Pequea — small river community, scenic overlook
  • Pairs well with the Holtwood loop
04
Cross-County · Half Day

Wrightsville–Columbia–York County Cross Ride

~65
Miles
2.5–3 hrs
Total Time

This route crosses the Susquehanna into York County, giving you a real taste of two counties in a single ride. Head west from Lancaster along the river road to Columbia, cross the bridge into Wrightsville, then explore the back roads of eastern York County before looping back.

York County's eastern townships — Hellam, Wrightsville, York Haven — are almost entirely devoid of tourist traffic and have excellent riding roads. Rolling hills, farm fields, and small river towns that haven't changed much in 50 years. The ride back across the Susquehanna at dusk is one of the better views in Central PA.

This is a great route if you want to extend your ride beyond Lancaster County without committing to a multi-hour highway run. It keeps you on two-lane roads the whole way.

Route Lancaster → PA-462 West → Columbia → Bridge to Wrightsville → PA-462 West → Hellam → York Haven → PA-83 South → Red Lion → PA-372 East → Peach Bottom → PA-372 East back toward Lancaster
Distance ~65 miles
Drive Time 2.5–3 hours with stops
Best For Two-county exploration, river crossings, back-road variety

Highlights

  • Columbia-Wrightsville Susquehanna bridge crossing
  • Eastern York County back roads — low traffic
  • York Haven and the lower river communities
  • Peach Bottom nuclear plant overlook on the river
05
Full County Loop · Full Day

Full Lancaster County Loop — Ephrata · Parkesburg · Holtwood · Marietta · Back

~110
Miles
4–5 hrs
Total Time

This is the big one — a full loop around Lancaster County that hits every terrain type: northern farmland, eastern Amish Country, southern river gorge, and western river towns. At 110 miles, it uses about a sixth of your 600-mile allowance and takes the better part of a day.

Starting north through Ephrata and Akron, you pass through some of the most productive farmland in the county before swinging east toward Parkesburg and Coatesville in Chester County. The southern leg drops into the Susquehanna gorge near Holtwood — the same section as Route 1 above, but approached from the east. Then you follow the river north through Marietta, one of the most well-preserved river towns in PA, and back into Lancaster to close the loop.

Plan for lunch in Marietta or along the river. This is the best full-day ride from Lancaster — and it rarely disappoints in any season. Fall color in October makes this route exceptional.

Route Lancaster → PA-272 North → Ephrata → Akron → PA-322 East → Parkesburg → PA-372 West → Quarryville → PA-272 South → Holtwood → River Road North → Safe Harbor → PA-441 North → Marietta → PA-23 East → Lancaster
Distance ~110 miles
Drive Time 4–5 hours with stops
Best For Full-day riders, all-terrain variety, fall foliage season

Highlights

  • Ephrata Cloister historic site (optional stop)
  • Holtwood Dam and Susquehanna gorge
  • Marietta — historic river town, great lunch stop
  • Covers all four quadrants of Lancaster County
  • Best in October for fall color
06
Covered Bridge Ride · Half Day

Lancaster North Loop — Lititz & Covered Bridge Back Roads

~45
Miles
2 hrs
Total Time

Lancaster County has more covered bridges than any other county in Pennsylvania — 29 at last count. The Northern Loop concentrates the highest density of them in a 45-mile circuit through Lititz, Rothsville, and the back roads of northern Lancaster County.

Lititz itself is worth a stop — it consistently ranks as one of Pennsylvania's coolest small towns. The downtown has a craft brewery, the world's only pretzel museum, and Sturgis Pretzel House (the oldest in America). From Lititz, the route continues north and east through covered bridge country on smaller township roads.

The bridges on this route include the Pinetown, Bucher's Mill, and Hunsecker Mill covered bridges — all single-lane wooden structures that date back to the 1800s. Some are open to traffic, some are pedestrian-only. The roads connecting them are the real reward: quiet, tree-shaded, and empty.

Route Lancaster → PA-501 North → Lititz → PA-772 East → Rothsville → Newport Road North → Covered Bridge Road → Hunsecker Road → PA-272 South → Lancaster
Distance ~45 miles
Drive Time 2 hours with stops
Best For Covered bridge enthusiasts, small-town stops, photo opportunities

Highlights

  • Lititz — craft brewery, Sturgis Pretzel House, walkable downtown
  • Pinetown Covered Bridge — over Muddy Creek
  • Hunsecker Mill Covered Bridge — one of the most photographed in PA
  • Northern Lancaster County township roads — quiet, tree-lined
